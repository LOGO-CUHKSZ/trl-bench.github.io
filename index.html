Overview

TRL-DLTE is the compositional suite of TRL-Bench. Encoders export frozen table, column, and row embeddings through their wrappers; shared lightweight heads then probe them across an end-to-end enrichment pipeline — so models from different training paradigms become directly comparable.

Encode once, reuse many

Tabular encoders promise reusable embeddings: encode a lake once, index it, and reuse across tasks where per-task fine-tuning is impractical. DLTE tests that promise at lake scale.

Three granularities, one task

Enrichment needs table embeddings to retrieve, column embeddings to align, and row embeddings to match — they must compose into a single end-to-end pipeline.

Composition is non-additive

The strongest pipelines are capability-matched hybrids, not single-encoder reuse — and per-stage marginal ranks do not determine the winning combination.

The DLTE Pipeline

Data-Lake Table Enrichment composes three embedding granularities into one end-to-end task. A pipeline may reuse a single multi-granular model or combine specialists across stages, making DLTE a composition test for the whole benchmark.

1

Table Retrieval

Retrieve candidate tables from the 47,772-table lake using table embeddings (target recall@100), against 36,740 CKAN distractors.

2

Column Alignment

Align columns and predict union / join / none using column embeddings, with thresholds calibrated on the development split.

3

Row Matching & Enrichment

Match rows across tables and merge content using row embeddings, recovering the removed cells end-to-end.

★

Scoring — UJ-H

End-to-end union/join recall via UJ-H (their harmonic mean), with Cell F1 as a complementary cell-recovery diagnostic.

The TRL-Bench protocol. Heterogeneous encoders are encoded once into row/column/table embeddings; shared probes evaluate them across CTbench, Rbench, and the compositional DLTE pipeline — Stage 1 retrieval → Stage 2 column alignment → Stage 3 row matching.

Key Findings

Once downstream conditions are standardized, encoder quality is capability-specific — and end-to-end quality depends on how stages compose, not on per-stage rank in isolation.

Hybrids beat monoliths

The best dev-selected pipeline, TUTA/GTE/GTE, reaches 0.229 UJ-H — 0.090 above the best monolithic BERT/BERT/BERT (0.139). The strongest pipelines combine capability-matched specialists rather than reuse a single encoder.

Marginals mislead

Per-stage marginal leaders assemble to just 0.134 (test) / 0.231 (dev) — a 0.097 swing. TABBIE leads the Stage-2 marginal yet never enters the top-50, where GTE and TURL dominate. Composition is non-additive.

Identity resolution transfers

DLTE Stage-3 row-matching rankings agree with atomic Record Linkage at Spearman |ρ|=0.80 (p=6.3×10−4), led by GTE and TransTab — a shared identity-resolution capability that persists across all four noise tiers.

Cell F1 vs. UJ-H across all 1,120 pipelines (test set; color = Stage-2 model, diamonds = monolithic). Capability-matched hybrids (e.g. Starmie/GTE/GTE, 0.253) sit well above single-encoder reuse, while the per-stage marginal assembly (Starmie/TABBIE/TransTab, 0.134) is far from the frontier.
